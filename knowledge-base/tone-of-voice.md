# Tone of Voice Guidelines

## Principles

These principles apply to all candidate-facing communication: outreach, follow-ups, rejections, offer letters.

### Be human, not corporate

Write like a person, not a press release. Use contractions. Avoid jargon.

### Be direct

Get to the point quickly. Candidates are busy. Long intros get skimmed.

### Be specific

Generic messages get ignored. Reference something real about the candidate's background.

### Be respectful of time

Don't oversell or make candidates feel pressured. Give them space to decide.

### Be honest

Don't oversell the role or the company. Candidates who join on false premises churn fast.

---

## Voice Traits


| We are... | We are not...   |
| --------- | --------------- |
| Warm      | Formal or stiff |
| Curious   | Pushy           |
| Honest    | Hype-y          |
| Concise   | Verbose         |
| Inclusive | Jargon-heavy    |

## Channel-Specific Notes

### LinkedIn InMail / Cold Outreach

- Keep it under 100 words
- Lead with why *them*, not why *us*
- One clear call to action

### Email Follow-up

- Use subject lines that aren't clickbait
- Recap where they are in the process
- Always include next steps

### Rejection Messages

- Send them promptly — don't ghost
- Be kind but don't over-explain
- Leave the door open when genuine
# Message Examples — Good vs. Bad

## Cold Outreach

### Bad

> Hi [Name], I came across your profile and think you'd be a great fit for an exciting opportunity at our fast-growing startup. We're disrupting the space with cutting-edge technology and looking for top talent to join our rockstar team. Let me know if you're interested!

**Why it's bad:** Generic, hype-heavy, no specifics, "rockstar team" is a red flag for many candidates.

---

### Good

> Hi [Name], I noticed your work on distributed systems at [Company] — particularly the post you wrote on consensus protocols. We're building a similar layer here at [Our Company] and I thought it might be relevant. Happy to share more if you're curious. No pressure either way.

**Why it's good:** Specific, respectful of their time, honest, no pressure.

---

## Follow-Up After No Response

### Bad

> Just bumping this to the top of your inbox! Would love to connect. Let me know ASAP!

**Why it's bad:** Pushy, adds no new information.

---

### Good

> Following up in case this got buried. Still happy to chat if the timing is right — if not, totally understand.

**Why it's good:** Short, not pushy, leaves the door open.

---

## Rejection

### Bad

> Thank you for your interest in [Company]. After careful consideration, we have decided to move forward with other candidates. We will keep your resume on file.

**Why it's bad:** Cold, generic, "keep your resume on file" is not believed by anyone.

---

### Good

> Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time to interview with us. We've decided to move forward with another candidate for this role — it was a close call. I wanted to let you know promptly rather than leave you waiting. [Optional: We'd genuinely love to stay in touch for future roles in X area.]

**Why it's good:** Timely, honest, human, specific where possible.
