# Recruiting Best Practices

## Dos

### Candidate Experience

- Respond to every application, even rejections
- Give a realistic preview of the role — including the hard parts
- Tell candidates what to expect at each stage before they experience it
- Start interviews on time
- Send a follow-up within 24 hours of every interview

### Outreach

- Always personalize — reference something specific about their background
- Lead with what's in it for them, not what you need
- Keep cold messages short (under 100 words)
- Follow up once; if no response after that, move on

### Interviews

- Share the interview format in advance
- Have a structured scorecard before the interview, not after
- Interviewers should not compare notes mid-loop — debrief together at the end
- Ask the same core questions to all candidates for the same role (fairness + comparison)

### Offers

- Move fast — don't let a great candidate wait more than 48 hours for an offer
- Deliver the offer verbally first, then in writing
- Be ready to explain every component of the comp package

---

## Don'ts

### Legal & Compliance

- Don't ask about age, marital status, religion, national origin, or family planning
- Don't make promises about future compensation, equity, or title that aren't in the offer
- Don't make hiring decisions based on anything not job-relevant

### Bias

- Don't penalize non-linear career paths without evidence it's relevant
- Don't equate "culture fit" with "people like us" — use defined value criteria
- Don't let one interviewer's strong opinion override the full panel without discussion
- Don't use school prestige as a proxy for ability

### Process

- Don't ghost candidates — ever
- Don't run 6+ interview rounds; respect candidates' time
- Don't change the job requirements after the process has started without resetting expectations
- Don't make candidates re-explain their background to every interviewer — share context internally

---

## Structured Interview Principles

1. Define the criteria before you start interviewing
2. Use the same questions for all candidates for a role
3. Score independently before the debrief
4. Separate "would I enjoy working with them" from "do they meet the bar"
5. Document your reasoning — not just your score

---

## On Speed

Hiring speed is a competitive advantage. Candidates who are in the market are often talking to multiple companies simultaneously.

- Application → first response: ≤ 3 business days
- Screen → next step: ≤ 5 business days
- Final loop → offer: ≤ 2 business days
- Offer → close: follow up within 24h if no response
